About Us

Welcome to our website dedicated to raising awareness and addressing the critical issue of land degradation. Land degradation, as defined by the United Nations Convention to Combat Desertification (UNCCD), is the result of human-induced actions that exploit land, causing its utility, biodiversity, soil fertility, and overall health to decline. With the growing human population and expanding agricultural activities, soils worldwide are being eroded up to 100 times faster than natural processes replenish them. Our website aims to provide valuable insights and practical solutions to combat land degradation. We focus on measuring soil pH levels, an essential parameter for soil health, as well as considering climatic conditions to determine the types of plants that can grow in specific regions. By doing so, we hope to promote sustainable land management practices and contribute to the global efforts towards Land Degradation Neutrality (LDN), a state whereby the amount and quality of land resources necessary to support ecosystem functions and services to enhance food security remain stable, or increase, within specified temporal and spatial scales and ecosystems Our website features articles, resources, and tools to help individuals, communities, and organizations understand the importance of healthy soils and the consequences of land degradation. We believe that by acknowledging soil for what it is and recognizing its irreplaceable role, we can change the way we care for it. By working together, we can prevent soil degradation, mitigate its effects, and ensure a sustainable future for all.
